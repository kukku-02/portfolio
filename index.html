I am Passionate About Breaking Records And Inspiring Others Through Sports.

Biography

My name is Deepthi Jeevanji, and I come from a small village called Kalleda in Telangana. Growing up with an intellectual disability wasn’t easy—people called me names and told my parents I would never achieve anything. But my parents believed in me. Despite our financial struggles, they supported my dream, even selling a piece of our land to fund my training. I discovered my talent for running in school, and with the help of my coach and the support of the Gopichand Foundation, I began training as a T20 para-athlete. Slowly, step by step, I started proving people wrong—not through words, but with my speed on the track.
In 2023, I won gold at the Asian Para Games in the 400m T20 event and set a new Asian record. Then in 2024, I broke the world record at the World Para Athletics Championships in Kobe, and later that year, I won a bronze medal at the Paris Paralympics—becoming the first Indian woman with an intellectual impairment to do so. Receiving the Arjuna Award was a moment I’ll never forget. I’ve now bought back the land my parents once sold, and I stand as proof that no limitation can define you. My journey is not just mine—it’s for every girl who’s ever been told she can’t.
